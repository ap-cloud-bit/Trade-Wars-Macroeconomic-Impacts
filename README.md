# Trade Wars and Macroeconomic Impacts
### A Multi‑Country Panel Data Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes the macroeconomic consequences of global trade wars using a multi‑country panel dataset spanning **2015–2025**.

The study focuses on how trade wars transmit through different economic channels, including:
- International trade flows
- GDP levels and growth
- Labor markets
- Foreign direct investment (FDI)

A combination of **exploratory data analysis**, **event‑study methods**, and **fixed‑effects regressions** is used to isolate trade‑war effects from global shocks such as the COVID‑19 pandemic.

---

## 📂 Data
- **Source:** World Bank & related international macroeconomic datasets
- **Countries:** Multiple advanced and emerging economies
- **Time Periods:**
  - Pre‑Trade War (2015–2017)
  - Trade War 1.0 (2018–2020)
  - Recovery (2021–2024)
  - Trade War 2.0 (2025, descriptive)

---

## 🔍 Methodology
1. Data cleaning and validation
2. Indicator‑level sanity checks
3. Exploratory data analysis (EDA)
4. Event‑study identification (baseline year: 2017)
5. Fixed‑effects panel regressions with robust standard errors

---

## 📊 Key Findings
- Trade wars did **not** cause a persistent decline in aggregate trade or GDP.
- GDP growth volatility increased, but level effects were limited.
- Labor markets were largely insulated until the pandemic shock.
- **Foreign direct investment shows the strongest and most persistent negative response to trade wars**, highlighting the role of policy uncertainty.
